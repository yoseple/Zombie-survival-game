# 9. Economy & Infrastructure Systems

## 9.1 Base Building System
The player's base is the physical manifestation of their progress and the primary sink for resources. It is viewed via a stylized isometric cutaway view.

### Shelter Progression
*   **Season 1:** Temporary camps (abandoned gas stations, caves). No permanent construction; focus is on barricades and trap placement.
*   **Season 2:** Mobile base (a fortified convoy or a hijacked train). Upgrades are limited to vehicle armor, storage capacity, and mobile medical bays.
*   **Season 3 & 4:** Permanent settlement. A massive, multi-tiered community requiring dedicated management.

### Key Facilities (Permanent Settlement)
*   **The Gates (Defense):** Upgraded from wood to reinforced steel. Dictates the settlement's base defense value against sieges and random horde events.
*   **Greenhouse (Food Production):** Reduces daily food consumption. Upgrading to Hydroponics makes the community entirely self-sufficient regarding base food needs.
*   **Water Filtration (Water Systems):** Purifies contaminated water. Failing to upgrade this results in random outbreaks of dysentery among the population, tanking Morale and Combat Effectiveness.
*   **Generator Room (Power Systems):** Requires constant Fuel. Provides power for advanced medical bays and defensive floodlights. 
*   **The Infirmary (Medical Facilities):** Allows passive healing of injured Companions between episodes. Upgrading to a Trauma Center allows curing early-stage infections.
*   **Shooting Range/Dojo (Training Facilities):** Allows inactive Companions to slowly level up their combat stats while the player is on missions.
*   **Recreation Area (Morale Systems):** A bar, library, or garden. Consumes Luxury Resources to provide a massive, passive Morale boost.

## 9.2 Resource System (The Economy of Scarcity)
*Dead Roads* employs a punishing economic model. There is never enough to feel completely safe.

### Core Resources
*   **Food (Rations/Canned/Fresh):** Consumed daily. Deficits cause starvation (health penalties) and massive Morale drops.
*   **Medicine (Bandages/Antibiotics/Trauma Kits):** Required to heal in combat and at the base. Highly scarce.
*   **Ammunition (Light/Heavy/Special):** The most precious commodity. Often used as currency in trades.
*   **Fuel (Gasoline/Diesel):** Required for generators and vehicles. Scavenged from abandoned cars.
*   **Building Materials (Scrap/Wood/Electronics):** Used solely for base upgrades and crafting weapons.
*   **Luxury Resources (Coffee, Cigarettes, Chocolate, Books):** Have no practical survival value but are essential for managing the PC's "Stress" meter and trading with high-end Factions.

### Economic Balancing & Scarcity
*   *Dynamic Scarcity:* If the player hoards 100 rounds of ammunition, the loot tables dynamically adjust to stop dropping ammo, forcing the player to use it or rely on other resources. 
*   *Trading:* Factions have fluctuating demands. In winter, the Iron Syndicate will pay double for Fuel but will not trade Food at any price. 

## 9.3 Inventory System
Inventory management is slot-based (like *Resident Evil 4*), forcing players to make hard choices before leaving the base.

### Categories
*   **Weapons:** Range from improvised (pipe wrenches) to military-grade. 
*   **Armor:** Kevlar vests, riot gear, or improvised tire-tread armor. Armor reduces incoming damage but heavily penalties mobility/AP.
*   **Consumables:** Molotovs, pipe bombs, medkits, stamina stimulants.

### Crafting & Degradation
*   **Degradation:** Melee weapons have durability. Firearms have a "Condition" stat; low condition drastically increases the chance of a jam (wasting an AP and the bullet).
*   **Crafting:** Conducted at the Base Workbench. Requires Blueprints (found via exploration) and specific Building Materials. 
    *   *Example:* Silencer = Scrap Metal + Duct Tape + Oil Filter + Gunsmith Blueprint.

### Loot Rarity & Progression
*   **Common (White):** Rusted pipes, moldy bread, empty bottles.
*   **Uncommon (Green):** Standard ammo, bandages, baseball bats.
*   **Rare (Blue):** Pristine firearms, trauma kits, antibiotics.
*   **Epic (Purple - "Artifacts"):** Unique weapons with lore attached (e.g., "Vance's Service Pistol" - Grants +10% Crit Chance against Federal Remnant). Cannot be crafted, only found or earned.
