# 5. Main Character System

The player character (PC) in *Dead Roads* is deeply customizable, not just aesthetically, but in how their past defines their capabilities and dialogue options in the apocalypse. 

## 5.1 Player Customization
*   **Visual Customization:** High-fidelity facial rigging. Players select gender, age, ethnicity, body type, and granular facial features (scars, tattoos, weathering).
*   **Voice Selection:** Three distinct voice actors per gender, offering different subtle inflections (e.g., Cynical, Earnest, Stoic).
*   **Dynamic Appearance:** As the seasons progress, the PC visually degrades. Clothing becomes tattered, hair grows, dirt accumulates, and permanent scars are added if the player takes critical damage in combat.

## 5.2 Backgrounds & Starting Professions
The player selects a "Pre-Fall Life" which dictates starting stats, unique dialogue options, and initial relationships.

*   **The First Responder (Paramedic/Firefighter):**
    *   *Starting Bonus:* High base Medicine and Stamina.
    *   *Dialogue Modifiers:* Can calm panicked survivors; possesses intrinsic medical knowledge during story events.
    *   *Starting Gear:* Trauma kit, Fire Axe.
*   **The White Collar (Lawyer/Corporate Exec):**
    *   *Starting Bonus:* High base Charisma and Negotiation.
    *   *Dialogue Modifiers:* Excels at manipulating others, detecting lies, and bartering.
    *   *Starting Gear:* High-end concealed carry pistol, pristine suit (high social value).
*   **The Blue Collar (Mechanic/Construction):**
    *   *Starting Bonus:* High base Crafting and Melee.
    *   *Dialogue Modifiers:* Can jury-rig solutions during exploration; identifies structural weaknesses.
    *   *Starting Gear:* Heavy wrench, reinforced work boots.
*   **The Veteran:**
    *   *Starting Bonus:* High base Firearms and Tactics.
    *   *Dialogue Modifiers:* Recognizes military tactics; commands immediate respect from ex-military NPCs.
    *   *Starting Gear:* Assault rifle (low ammo), tactical vest.
*   **The Outcast (Criminal/Drifter):**
    *   *Starting Bonus:* High base Stealth and Survival.
    *   *Dialogue Modifiers:* Understands the criminal underworld; adept at lockpicking and intimidation.
    *   *Starting Gear:* Shiv, lockpick set.

## 5.3 Personality Archetypes
Dialogue choices actively shape the PC's "Archetype" on a dynamic spectrum. The game tracks thousands of micro-decisions to place the player into one of four quadrants:

1.  **The Pragmatist (Logic & Ruthlessness):** Prioritizes survival above all. Will sacrifice the weak to save the strong. Unlocks intimidation dialogue.
2.  **The Idealist (Empathy & Morality):** Prioritizes human life and rebuilding society. Unlocks inspiring speeches and peaceful conflict resolution.
3.  **The Autocrat (Control & Order):** Believes survival requires absolute discipline. Unlocks command directives and reduces mutiny chance through fear.
4.  **The Lone Wolf (Independence & Stealth):** Trusts no one but themselves. Unlocks stealth-based narrative bypasses and self-preservation dialogue.

## 5.4 Skill Systems & Progression
Progression in *Dead Roads* is strictly use-based and narrative-driven, abandoning traditional "XP" for a more organic system. 

*   **Combat Skills (Firearms, Melee, Tactics):** Improve slightly through use in combat, but major upgrades require finding "Manuals" or being trained by skilled Companions (e.g., spending downtime with the Veteran companion increases Firearms).
*   **Survival Skills (Scavenging, Stealth, Crafting):** Improve by interacting with the environment. Crafting higher-tier items requires upgrading the base workbench.
*   **Social Skills (Negotiation, Intimidation, Empathy):** Improve by engaging in deep dialogue. Repeatedly using Intimidation makes it more effective but locks out Empathy options.

## 5.5 Leadership Systems
The PC is thrust into leadership. Their ability to lead is governed by a complex set of hidden mechanics.

*   **Command Points (CP):** Generated by high Morale and the PC's Charisma stat. Used during combat to issue "Directives" (e.g., granting an ally a free attack) or during base management to rush construction.
*   **The "Weight of the Crown" Mechanic:** Every hard decision increases the PC's "Stress." If Stress caps out, the PC suffers a nervous breakdown, resulting in penalties to all stats for a full episode and a temporary loss of CP. Stress is mitigated by spending downtime with trusted companions, consuming luxury items (alcohol, cigars), or listening to music artifacts.
*   **Delegation:** The PC cannot manage everything. They must appoint Companions to roles (Quartermaster, Chief of Security, Head Medic). The effectiveness of these roles depends on the assigned Companion's skills and their Trust in the PC. Assigning the wrong person can lead to disaster.
