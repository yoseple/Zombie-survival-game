# 6. Survivor & Relationship Systems

## 6.1 The 50 Major Survivors (Roster Architecture)
*Dead Roads* features a massive cast of 50 unique, fully-realized "Major Survivors" that can join the player's community over the four seasons. Each has bespoke voice acting, personal questlines, and unique mechanical benefits. 

### Roster Selection (Key Examples of the 50)
1.  **Elias (The Cynical Sniper):** *Trait:* Dead Eye. *Loyalty:* Hard to earn. *Fear:* Starvation. *Ambition:* Solitude.
2.  **Dr. Aris Thorne (The Broken Surgeon):** *Trait:* Master Healer. *Loyalty:* Requires ethical leadership. *Fear:* The Infected. *Ambition:* Finding a cure.
3.  **Maya (The Feral Child):** *Trait:* Uncanny Stealth. *Loyalty:* Attached to the PC. *Fear:* Enclosed spaces. *Ambition:* Vengeance.
4.  **Jackson (The Ex-Cop):** *Trait:* Pacifier. *Loyalty:* Values law/order. *Fear:* Chaos. *Ambition:* Rebuilding government.
5.  **Sarah (The Grief-Stricken Mother):** *Trait:* Desperate Strength. *Loyalty:* Highly volatile. *Fear:* Losing anyone else. *Ambition:* Creating a safe haven.
6.  **"Boomer" (The Explosives Expert):** *Trait:* Demolitions. *Loyalty:* Easily bought with luxury items. *Fear:* Quiet. *Ambition:* Watching things burn.
7.  **Reverend Thomas (The Zealous Priest):** *Trait:* Inspiring Orator. *Loyalty:* Requires faith-based choices. *Fear:* Damnation. *Ambition:* Spiritual conquest.
8.  **Chloe (The Tech Prodigy):** *Trait:* Jury-Rigger. *Loyalty:* Needs intellectual stimulation. *Fear:* EMPs. *Ambition:* Restoring the grid.
9.  **Garrison (The Former Raider):** *Trait:* Brutal Melee. *Loyalty:* Respects strength. *Fear:* Weakness. *Ambition:* Becoming second-in-command.
10. **Elena (The Botanist):** *Trait:* Green Thumb (Boosts base food). *Loyalty:* Easy to earn. *Fear:* Fire. *Ambition:* Cultivating a self-sustaining farm.
*(Note: The remaining 40 survivors follow this exact rigorous matrix of Trait, Loyalty Condition, Fear, and Ambition, ensuring systemic variety).*

## 6.2 The Deep Social Simulation
Survivors are governed by an intricate web of hidden and visible variables.

### The Variables
*   **Trust Score (-100 to +100):** Determines if they obey orders in combat and if they will confide in the player during camp scenes.
*   **Morale (0 to 100):** Affected by base conditions, recent deaths, and food rations. Low morale leads to desertion or suicide.
*   **Fear Factor:** Each character has a specific trigger (e.g., darkness, specific infected variants). Encountering these triggers drastically reduces combat effectiveness.
*   **Interpersonal Chemistry:** Survivors have hidden affinities for each other. (e.g., Elias hates Jackson's rigid rules; Maya looks up to Sarah as a mother figure). 

### Recruitment and Permanent Consequences
Recruitment is rarely a simple "join me" dialogue. It requires completing specific, often dangerous, tasks. Once recruited, a survivor is a permanent part of the simulation until they die.
*   **Death is Permanent:** There are no resurrection mechanics. If Dr. Thorne dies in Season 1, the community suffers a massive medical penalty for the rest of the game.
*   **The Mourning Mechanic:** When a survivor dies, their friends suffer a massive Morale penalty. If their enemy dies, their Morale might actually *increase*.

## 6.3 Relationship Mechanics
*   **The Memory System:** The game logs interactions. If the player denies a survivor extra rations in Episode 2, that survivor may refuse to cover the player's flank in a firefight in Episode 4.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** Emergent camp events occur where two survivors clash. The player must intervene. 
    *   *Arbitration:* Taking a side boosts trust with one and damages the other.
    *   *Neutrality:* Refusing to intervene damages trust with both but prevents an immediate fight.
*   **Betrayal & Mutiny:** If a survivor's Trust hits -100 and their Morale is low, they will not just leave; they will steal supplies, sabotage the base, or actively try to murder the player in their sleep.
*   **Romance:** Romance is a slow-burn mechanic. It requires consistent alignment with the survivor's values, protecting them, and choosing specific vulnerable dialogue options during "Campfire Scenes." Romancing a character grants a massive buff when fighting alongside them ("Lovers' Adrenaline") but creates extreme vulnerability; if one dies, the other may suffer a total psychological break.
*   **Friendship/Rivalry:** Companions form bonds independently. The player can foster these by assigning them to work details together. Strong friendships unlock "Combo Attacks" in turn-based combat.

## 6.4 Leadership and Influence
The player cannot control every action of their survivors outside of direct combat.
*   **Autonomy:** High-trust survivors will autonomously gather resources or fortify the base during downtime. Low-trust survivors will slack off or hoard items for themselves.
*   **The Council:** Once the community reaches a certain size, the player must form a "Council" of three trusted advisors. The composition of the Council heavily influences the "Flavor" of the base (Militaristic, Democratic, Pragmatic) and dictates the passive bonuses the community receives.
