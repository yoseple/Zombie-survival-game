# 3. World Design

## 3.1 The Universe & Outbreak Origin
**The Strain: "The Chimera Pathogen" (Ophiocordyceps Humanus + Rabies Lyssavirus Variant)**
The world of *Dead Roads* is grounded in a terrifying, scientifically plausible collapse. The apocalypse is not supernatural; it is ecological and biological. The outbreak is caused by a synthetic, hybridized pathogen created not as a bioweapon, but as an accidental byproduct of radical climate change intervention experiments. 

In an attempt to engineer a viral agent capable of breaking down indestructible micro-plastics in the world's oceans, a rogue corporate research facility (Apex BioSolutions, based in the Pacific Northwest) spliced a hyper-aggressive fungal enzyme with a fast-acting viral delivery system. The resulting "Chimera Pathogen" mutated rapidly in the wild. It spread through contaminated water supplies, remaining dormant in human hosts for months before a specific environmental trigger (a sudden spike in global temperatures) caused simultaneous, mass-activation.

The pathogen aggressively rewrites the host's central nervous system, prioritizing transmission via biting and scratching. It dramatically increases adrenaline production, making the infected incredibly fast, strong, and highly resistant to pain, while slowly calcifying their epidermal layer, making them tougher to kill over time.

## 3.2 The Timeline of the Collapse
*   **Day 0: The Activation.** Millions of people worldwide "turn" simultaneously. Hospitals are immediately overrun as patients exhibit hyper-aggression. 
*   **Day 3: The Blackout.** Grid failures begin as maintenance crews are killed or abandon their posts. Communication networks become localized and unreliable. The government declares martial law.
*   **Day 7: The "Red Zones."** Major population centers are officially classified as unsalvageable. The military begins firebombing cities in a desperate, futile attempt to contain the spread.
*   **Day 14: The Splintering.** Chain of command breaks down. Military units desert to protect their own families or establish warlord states. The federal government effectively ceases to exist.
*   **Month 3: The Silent Winter.** The first winter post-outbreak. Millions die not from the infected, but from starvation, freezing, and easily preventable diseases. The weak are culled.
*   **Year 1 (Game Start):** The initial panic has subsided. The world is now in a state of grim equilibrium. Survivors have formed hardened enclaves, and the infected have begun to mutate into specialized, localized variants based on their environment.

## 3.3 Regions and Geography
The narrative spans a massive geographic journey across the devastated United States, divided into distinct, highly detailed regions:

### The Pacific Northwest (The Cradle)
*   **Atmosphere:** Dense, perpetually damp, towering redwoods, constant fog. Nature is aggressively reclaiming the urban spaces.
*   **Hazards:** "Spore Pockets" in damp valleys that cause hallucinations and respiratory failure if inhaled without a mask. 
*   **Key City: Seattle Ruins.** A flooded, vertical maze. The Space Needle has collapsed, creating a massive bridge across flooded districts. 

### The High Desert (The Scorch)
*   **Atmosphere:** Blistering heat by day, freezing by night. Arid, unforgiving terrain with vast stretches of nothingness.
*   **Hazards:** Dehydration, heatstroke, and "Sand-Walkers" (infected whose flesh has mummified in the heat, making them armored and virtually silent).
*   **Key City: Salt Lake City.** Now a fortified religious stronghold controlled by a zealot faction who believe the infected are a divine test.

### The Rust Belt (The Iron Graveyard)
*   **Atmosphere:** Decaying industrial infrastructure, rusted factories, polluted rivers, grey skies.
*   **Hazards:** Chemical leaks, unstable structures, and highly militarized bandit factions fighting over scrap metal and fuel refineries.
*   **Key City: Detroit.** A massive fortress city divided into warring fiefdoms, where ammunition is the only currency.

## 3.4 Major Factions (Overview)
*   **The Federal Remnant (F.E.D.R.A. Equivalent):** Remnants of the military trying to restore order through brutal authoritarianism. They control the last working helicopters and heavy artillery.
*   **The Sowers:** An agrarian cult that worships the Chimera Pathogen as a necessary cleansing of the Earth. They use infected as guard dogs and deliberate biological weapons.
*   **The Iron Syndicate:** A massive trading coalition that controls the old interstate highways using armored convoys. They are ruthless capitalists; everything has a price.
*   **The Free States:** A loose, democratic coalition of smaller settlements trying to rebuild the old world. Plagued by bureaucracy and internal division.

## 3.5 Major Locations
*   **Apex Facility Site Alpha:** The epicenter of the outbreak. A massive underground complex now overrun by the most mutated and terrifying variants of the infected.
*   **The Hoover Dam (The Spark):** The last major functioning power source in the West. A constant warzone between the Federal Remnant and the Iron Syndicate.
*   **The Safe Zone (Eden):** A mythical settlement in the Canadian Rockies rumored to be entirely free of the infected, driving much of the Season 1 & 2 narrative propulsion.

## 3.6 Lore and Historical Events (Post-Collapse)
*   **The Massacre of I-5:** A tragic event where the military panicked and opened fire on a miles-long traffic jam of refugees fleeing Seattle, creating a massive zone of the dead that is still impassable.
*   **The Night of Torches:** An event where a settlement known as New Hope was betrayed from within and burned to the ground by The Sowers. Survivors of this event are scattered and deeply traumatized.
*   **The Great Silence:** A period of three weeks during Month 6 where all radio frequencies suddenly went dead across the continent, sparking rumors of an EMP or extraterrestrial involvement (later revealed to be a massive solar flare compounding the misery).

## 3.7 Environmental Storytelling
*Dead Roads* relies heavily on showing, not telling. 
*   **The Tragic Dioramas:** A child's bedroom where the toys are barricading the door. A pair of skeletons holding hands on a park bench, empty poison vials at their feet. A blood-stained nursery.
*   **Graffiti and Warnings:** Messages scrawled on walls serve as both lore and gameplay hints. "DON'T MAKE A SOUND," "THE WATER IS POISON," or "THEY COME OUT AT NIGHT."
*   **The "Artifacts":** Collectible items that are entirely mundane—a broken watch, a faded photograph, an old MP3 player—but possess deeply emotional audio diaries or text logs that humanize the people who lived and died before the player arrived.
